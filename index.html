Who I Am

About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Department of Physical Sciences, Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru, working at the intersection of quantum photonics and two-dimensional materials.

My doctoral work at IISER Mohali & INST Mohali focused on surface and interface engineering of van der Waals 2D materials — from de-wrinkling black phosphorus with electron beams to sculpting artificial edges for piezoelectric responses.

I combine cutting-edge nanofabrication (EBL, RIE) with optical and scanning probe techniques (AFM/PFM/KPFM, low-temperature Raman, PL, TRPL, g² measurements) to advance the field of quantum light sources in solid-state systems.

What I Work On

Current Research

Single Photon Emission

Coupling photonic cavities and plasmonics to achieve high-brightness, ultra-pure single-photon emission in 2D semiconductors.

Quantum Photonics

Electron Beam & Transport

Studying the effect of electron beam irradiation on transport properties of 2D materials via FET measurements.

Nanoelectronics

Raman Vibrational Modes

Exploring variations in Raman modes of 2D materials and their heterostructures under electric field and strain.

Spectroscopy

Dipole–Dipole Interactions

Selectively enhancing weaker exciton emission via resonant dipole–dipole interaction in hybrid 2D heterojunctions.

Excitonic Physics

Bolometric Response

Room-temperature bolometric response in RGO-Chitosan hybrids via defect-assisted electron–phonon scattering.

Sensors

Biomarker Detection

Detecting sepsis biomarkers through Raman spectroscopy signature peak identification.

Bio-Nanotechnology
